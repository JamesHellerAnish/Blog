Hot and Burning IT skills and how to learn them

Home Blog Hot and Burning IT skills and how to learn them

What is some IT skill that I should learn? Would it help me excel in my career?

These are some questions that may be going through your mind if you are an IT professional or a beginner in a technical field or maybe a computer science student who is looking for a skill to start their career or just an enthusiast who would like to be updated about all the things going around. Well, you landed on the right page, here you will learn about some new, hot and burning skills that will surely help you.

If you are here because you are bored to death because of the lockdown and finally made up your mind to learn some new skills. Luck is on your side today, you surely will learn about some interesting skills to start and also how to learn them. Some website's that will help you learn and master the skills of your choice. Stay tuned.

So before we start with the skills we should know what does the workforce wants? Which skills were more trending in the past year and which will be on the top list of the employers this year and maybe the years to come because you wouldn't want to keep jumping from one skill to another as it comes, it will be too hectic and because there are new languages or tools that comes every six months so learning all of them is not apt.

These are some IT skills that you should consider going through before you decide on learning any new skills,

  1. Cloud computing

    Amazon AWS one of the leaders of cloud computing defines cloud computing as,cloud computing is one of the most on-demand deliveries of IT resources over the Internet which has a pay-as-you-go pricing system. With the use of it, you can access technology services, such as computing power, storage, and databases, on an as-needed basis from a cloud provider instead of buying, owning, and maintaining physical data centres and servers.

    Each and every Organization rather small, big or industries are shifting to clouds for their day to day operations from data backup to real-time fraud detection and prevention and everything in between. So getting into the cloud is very beneficial now to grow your career or to start it as a beginner. If you go pay wise it is one of the highest-paid jobs in the IT sector according to data in 2019. You must have certifications on various cloud computing features ranges from developing the cloud until the security and architecture. You can do Microsoft certifications for better job opportunities in this sector.

  2. Cybersecurity

    As the name suggests it deals with security of systems, servers over the internet or in an organisation. As the internet grows the threat from it to the individual as well as the organisation grows exponentially. Companies are hiring cybersecurity professional at a higher wage as there is more demand for this sector than supply. Not anyone can become a cybersecurity expert. One should have a mindset, dedication to becoming one. And once you are there no one can stop you from achieving success in this field.

    Not many websites offer this course at an expert level but you can start with the basics from any website and then get certified. Some websites like Udemy, cybrary will help you get started on this journey. So if you have what it takes to become a cybersecurity expert go for it.

  1. Blockchain

    Blockchain was born in 2009 in order to support the use of cryptocurrency. It is a simple but ingenious way of passing information from point A to point B. The transfer of information is transparent, automated and safe. It is mostly used by banks, financial sectors, etc. Blockchain is beneficial for the financial sectors to safely authorize and transfer digital assets. Blockchainâ€™s unique way to store, validate, authorize and shift data across the internet has evolved to securely store and send any digital asset. The infrastructure cost of blockchain is high but there is no transaction cost. Every instance of a block is verified by millions of computers or nodes so falsifying data in a blockchain means falsifying the whole chain. It has a vast future. There is a small supply of professionals who have this skill set and they are in high demand. So learning this skill is a career booster.

    Coursera offers the course on blockchain. There are 20+ certifications available on the blockchain.

  2. AI and machine learning

    Artificial intelligence (AI) augments the capabilities of the human workforce. With the rapid shift of industries to cloud platforms, it is mandatory for programmers or developers to also shift to a platform which will help them to easily migrate to the cloud-based platforms and they can do that by automating the process with the help of AI and machine learning. The people who can harness the power of AI, machine learning and natural language processing are the ones who will help organizations deliver more relevant, personalized, and innovative products and services.

    The list we have given above is not a top list of skills. It is just a tip of the iceberg, just a shortlist to get you started on the skills. When you dive down in any of the topics mentioned above you will know what a vast ocean it is. In cloud computing, there are 8 different certifications which are further divided into 3 different levels- beginner, intermediate and advanced. So if you are a fresher you have a long way to go but remember that with every certification you will learn and grow. You can consider learning any skill you think would be better for your resume or career and which comes under your forte.

    There are many websites that provide resources and will help you learn these skills from beginner to expert level. Websites like Udemy, Khan Academy, Coursera, cybrary, and many free videos are also available on youtube.

Hope it would help you to choose a better career for yourself.
