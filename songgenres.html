Music genres to go for

Music genres and sub genres can be classified based on many factors like the instrument being played, the way an instrument is played, the speed of the music, loudness of the vocals, loudness of the instruments also different rhythm leads to different categories of music. Almost all the time these categories overlap and it give rise to various controversies. Each song has its own style and form. Some songs can have same style but they can be of different forms or some can have same forms but they can be of different styles. The genres and sub genres can be categorised based on these styles and forms. Origin of the music plays a very important role in categorising a genre, the origin may be geographical or some traditional context like music that came into picture during renaissance period. Music can also be categorised by the musical techniques, content and the cultural context. People listen to or like similar songs (same style or form) of different genres this can be explained by the three dimensions of arousal, valence and depth. According to musicology, arousal, valence and depth are the three terms which can be used to assess the music and also to classify it as a genre and sub genre. Arousal refers to physiological processes such as stimulation and relaxation (intense, forceful, abrasive, thrilling vs. gentle, calming, mellow), valence refers to emotion and mood processes (fun, happy, lively, enthusiastic, joyful vs. depressing, sad), and depth refers to cognitive processes (intelligent, sophisticated, inspiring, complex, poetic, deep, emotional, thoughtful vs. party music, danceable).

In a general way music can be classified as Art music, Folk music, Popular music and Religious music. Based on the occasion or mood the choice of music can change.

Art Music:

Art music includes historical classical music form and contemporary music. Art music exists all over the world. Music in this category is mostly in writing form rather than in recording. It requires focused attention from the listener to understand all the criticism and detailed and technical deconstruction that it invites. In this category, the work or piece of music is associated with the composer rather than the performer and it usually defined by noted versions.

Folk Music:

In this category, usually the composer or the artist is not known, and there may exists several different versions of the same song. In this genre, usually music is orally passed from one generation to another. Popular folk songs is disseminated by singing, listening and dancing to it. The styles (pitches and cadences) as well as the context of the culture it had developed is transmitted through it. Culturally transmitting folk songs in this way maintain the rich evidence about the period of history when the songs were created and the society in which it was developed. The musical instruments that is used while singing this folk songs also differs from place to place, some of these instruments like piano, accordion, various types of flutes and trumpets, banjo have found their place and is famous worldwide. Every country in the world has its own folk song, in some cases each region, district and community, has its own folk music style. The place, cultural identity and history that are developed by each, plays an important role in different sub-divisions of folk genre.

Religious Music:

Religious Music or also known as sacred music are composed and performed for religious use. spiritual music often includes gospels, bhajans, mantras and aartis in India, christmas musics.

Popular Music:

Popular music or Pop is a musical style which is accessible to the general public, unlike art music it is conceived for the mass distribution. This type of music is listened by socioculturally heterogeneous groups and disseminated by the mass media. Popular music are available on radios, commercial and public service, with music retailers, in department stores. Many characters like composer, songwriter, singer and most importantly producer is required from creation to production of a song.

Rock, Electronic, soul or Rhythm & Blues (R&B), Funk, Country, Latin, Reggae, Hip Hop, Polka are all the music genres with in pop category.

Rock Music:

Rock music is originated in early 1950s in the United States. Initially string instruments were used in the rock but slowly started including all types of instruments. Rock is centred around electric guitar with bass, drum and typically a singer forms a band group. In a nutshell, rock music is a loud music and one of the reason it is popular among youths. The term Rock concert came into picture where the rock band (group of people who specialises in a particular regional instrument form a band and perform together) performs live on stage in front thousands of people. These concerts made them more popular among the folks. The rock bands like metallica, nirvana, megadeth, pink floyds are some of the top bands. Rock music also incorporates from jazz, electric blues, folk, classical and other music styles.

The sub genres of rock music includes Acid rock alternative rock, arena rock, art rock, beat music, Christian rock, experimental rock, garage rock, glam rock, hard rock, heartland rock, heavy metal, indie rock, new wave, occult rock, post-punk, post-rock, progressive rock, psychedelic rock, punk rock, roots rock, soft rock, stoner rock, surf music. And some of the fusion genres are blues rock, country rock, dance rock, electronic rock, folk rock, funk rock, industrial rock, jazz rock.

Electronic Music:

Electronic music refers to electronically influenced music, it uses electronic musical instrument, digital instruments. Electronic music is one of the most famous genre of music created by DJs by combining multiple songs with different tunes combining it to form a unique music. Nowadays almost all the dance club plays this genre of music as it is adored by most of the people. Many live concerts and competition between DJs are arranged for the entertainment of people. The sub genres of music includes EDM, chip tune, drum and bass, electro, electroacoustic, glitch, electronica, techno, trance, synth- pop, industrial, jungle, house. There are many different genres which are fused with electronic named as fusion genre which includes electropop, electronic rock, electro punk, trip hop, tech house. 

 

Soul music/ Rhythm and Blues:

R&B music is a combination of jazz and blues. It is the modern version of the soul and funk influenced by the African American pop music. Singers like Rihanna, Beyoncé, Usher and the legendary Michael Jackson have all made it huge in this music industry.

Hip Hop music:

Hip Hop also referred to as rap music is originally came from United States in 1970s, specifically from New York City by the African American community. Nowadays it is the top on the music list. Hip Hop music consists of four elements, rapping, break dancing, graffiti and disk jockeying. The different sub genres include free style rap, gangsta rap, hard core hip hop, alternative hip hop, nerdcore, etc.
